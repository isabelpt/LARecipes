Chocotorta
(Argentina)

Since Argentina is in the Andes Mountains it has a warmer climate and low lands which makes it one of the biggest food-producing countries. Native groups in Argentina farmed melons, squash, and sweet potatoes. It has Spanish, European, Italian, British, German, Jewish, and other immigrants settlers influencing lots of dishes.

Directions

Filling

  1. Use 16 ounces of cream cheese (two 8-ounce packages) and 2 cups of dulce de leche, which should give you about 500 grams (17.64 ounces) of each.
  2. Blend your dulce de leche and your cream cheese together using an electric mixer. This step will be easier if you allow your cream cheese to warm up to room temperature before mixing.
  3. Dip your chocolate biscuits in the coffee and set them out on your cake platter. Arrange them to make your first layer.

Assemble

  1. Add your filling to a pastry bag (the type used for frosting a cake).
  2. Squeeze out dollops of filling all around the edges on the first layer of cookies, which will give your cake a decorative look.
  3. Fill the middle of your layer with more filling and smooth it out.
  4. Add the next layer of coffee-soaked cookies and repeat the process of adding the filling. Continue like this until you have four layers of cookies and three layers of filling.

Finishing the Cake

  1. Cover your cake and place it in the refrigerator for at least four hours, which gives the cookies a chance to absorb some of the moisture and flavor of this filling.
